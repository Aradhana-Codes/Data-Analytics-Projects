Retail Orders Analysis

Overview: This project analyzes 9994 retail orders data to understand sales performance, profit trends, customer behavior, and operational efficiency.
The goal is to identify revenue drivers, loss-making areas, discount impact, and growth opportunities using an end-to-end data analytics workflow.

Dataset: 
Source: Kaggle – Retail Orders Dataset 
Format: CSV 
Total Records: 9994 orders 
Time Period: 2022–2023 
Features:16 columns

Tools & Technologies: 
1 Python: Kaggle API data extraction, Data loading and preprocessing
2 SQL (MySQL): Aggregations, KPI calculations
3 Power BI: Interactive dashboards and visual analytics
4 Jupyter Notebook: Analysis documentation

Key Results & Insights: 
Overall Profit Margin is 9.26%, indicating moderate profitability with room for improvement.Revenue shows seasonal monthly fluctuations across 2022–2023. Technology and Furniture categories contribute higher profit margins. High discounts are strongly associated with lower or negative profit, confirmed through scatter analysis.Bottom 5 products by profit consistently generate losses, highlighting cost and pricing issues. Regional analysis shows West and East regions outperform others in revenue contribution.

Business Insights:
Discount optimization can significantly improve profitability.
Loss-making products require pricing, cost, or assortment review.
Focusing on high-margin categories and regions can maximize business returns.
Profit Margin KPI adds deeper business understanding beyond revenue alone.

Conclusion:
This project demonstrates Automated data extraction using Python & Kaggle API, Strong SQL querying skills, Effective Power BI dashboarding and Ability to convert raw retail data into actionable business insights.
