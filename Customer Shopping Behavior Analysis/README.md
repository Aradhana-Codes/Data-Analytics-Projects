 Customer Shopping Behavior Analysis

Overview:
This project analyzes 3,900 customer purchase transactions to uncover insights into shopping behavior, customer segments, product performance, and revenue drivers. 
The analysis follows a complete end-to-end data analytics workflow, combining Python, SQL, Power BI, and business-focused reporting to support strategic decision-making.

Dataset:
Format: CSV
Total Records: 3,900 transactions
Features:18 columns

Tools & Technologies:
Python (Pandas, NumPy) – Data loading,cleaning
MySQL – Structured SQL analysis and querying
Power BI – Interactive dashboards and visual storytelling
Gamma – Professional presentation (PPT) creation
Jupyter Notebook – Analysis and documentation


Key Results & Insights:
Male customers generate 2.1x more revenue than female customers.
Young Adult and Middle-aged customers are the highest revenue contributors.
Clothing category drives the most revenue, followed by Accessories.
Express shipping users show slightly higher average spending.
High-value customers continue to spend well even when using discounts.
Strong opportunity to convert repeat non-subscribers into subscribers.


Conclusion: 
This project demonstrates practical skills in data analysis, SQL, dashboarding, and business communication. 
It reflects real-world analytics workflows and showcases the ability to transform raw data into actionable business insights.
